"India's rising population and vehicular ownership have led to problems such as traffic congestion, pollution, and road accidents. The transportation sector is a crucial player in the country's economy, but at the same time, it is also a significant contributor to climate change", this a key reason as to which why India's economy sees constant growth but its sustainability factor doesn't which is a problem that must be solved. Moreover, the unsustainable transport system in India puts a significant strain on its economic health, absorbing a considerable portion of household incomes and public funds. The environmental impact is profound, with transport being a major source of greenhouse gas emissions, contributing to global warming and health hazards from air pollution. Safety is another critical concern, as inadequate road conditions and poorly managed traffic lead to a high number of accidents and fatalities annually. To mitigate these problems, India needs a holistic overhaul of its transport strategy, emphasizing sustainability, efficiency, and inclusivity. this includes promoting electric vehicles, enhancing pedestrian and cycling infrastructure, and leveraging technology for smarter traffic management. Encouraging public participation in planning and decision-making processes can also ensure that solutions are community-driven and sustainable. Integrating regional transport plans with urban development policies is crucial to address the sprawling urbanization and its transport needs effectively.
